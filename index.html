Biography

I graduated from UCSD with a degree in bioinformatics, where I discovered the power of computational and statistical methods in genomic analysis. This passion led me to Illumina, a leading sequencing company, where I applied my skills as a software engineer and learned about sequencing pipelines. Motivated by this experience, I pursued a Ph.D. in Computational Biology at USC to deepen my expertise.

In my Ph.D. research, I aimed to connect DNA sequence analysis and structural biology, focusing on how high-throughput DNA analysis and 3D structural insights into protein interactions are essential for developing therapeutics. I honed my skills in Molecular Dynamics (MD) simulations, contributing to two published papers on protein-DNA interactions.

Recently, advancements in protein structure prediction using machine learning inspired me to incorporate Deep Learning and Generative Models into my work. Collaborating with my colleagues, I used these tools to analyze protein-DNA and protein-ligand complexes, leading to a publication in Nature Methods. I find this process of scientific inquiry enlightening and gratifying, and I look forward to our upcoming publication.
